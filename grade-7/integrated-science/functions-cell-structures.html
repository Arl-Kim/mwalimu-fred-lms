Functions of the Cell Structures found in Plant and Animal Cells
Part/Structure Function Found In
Vacuole It is the space that contains a watery fluid (cell sap) with dissolved water, mineral salts, and waste products. Both plant and animal cells
Cell Membrane A thin layer around the cell that holds it together. It controls what goes in and out of the cell, allowing it to take in necessary substances and get rid of waste products. Both plant and animal cells
Cytoplasm A jelly-like liquid filling the inside of the cell. It is where chemical reactions occur and contains organelles that perform specific functions. Both plant and animal cells
Nucleus Carries genetic information and controls all the activities of the cell. Both plant and animal cells
Cell Wall A thick, tough layer made of cellulose, found outside the cell membrane in plant cells. It helps maintain cell shape and protects the cell from mechanical damage. Plant cells
Chloroplast An organelle in the cytoplasm of plant cells containing chlorophyll, a green pigment that absorbs light for photosynthesis. Plant cells
