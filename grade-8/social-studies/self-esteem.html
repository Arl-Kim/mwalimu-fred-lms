TOPIC 2: BUILDING SELF ESTEEM
  • Self-esteem is how we value and perceive ourselves. It's based on our opinions and beliefs about ourselves.
  • Building self-esteem refers to the process of developing a positive perception and belief in one’s own worth and abilities.
  • It involves recognizing and appreciating one’s strengths, accomplishments, and unique qualities while accepting and learning from mistakes and setbacks.

Factors that influence self-esteem in day-to-day life:
  • Personal Achievements - When you accomplish something you've been working towards, such as getting a good grade, winning a game, or completing a project, it can boost your self-esteem. Celebrating your achievements reminds you of your capabilities and builds confidence.
  • Positive Feedback - Receiving positive feedback from others, whether it's from friends, family, or teachers, can make you feel valued and appreciated. Compliments and words of encouragement can have a positive impact on your self-esteem.
  • Supportive Relationships - Having supportive and caring relationships with friends and family members who believe in you can greatly influence your self-esteem. When you have people who encourage and support you, it helps you feel more confident and secure in yourself.
  • Comparing Yourself to Others - Comparing yourself to others, especially in terms of looks, abilities, or achievements, can negatively affect your self-esteem. Remember that everyone is unique, and it's important to focus on your own progress and growth rather than comparing yourself to others.
  • Unrealistic Beauty Standards - Unrealistic beauty standards and expectations can lead to low self-esteem. The media, including social media platforms, can shape your perception of beauty, success, and worth. It's essential to be mindful of how media portrays unrealistic standards and to remember that everyone is unique in their own way.
  • Self-Criticism - Being overly critical of yourself can erode your self-esteem. It's important to practice self-compassion and treat yourself with kindness. Instead of focusing on your flaws, try to embrace your strengths and accept yourself for who you are.
  • Bullying or Negative Experiences - Experiencing bullying or negative interactions can significantly impact your self-esteem. It's crucial to seek support from trusted adults and address these situations. Remember that the hurtful words or actions of others do not define your worth.
  • Positive Interactions - Positive interactions such as showing empathy and acts of kindness can boost our self-esteem while negative interactions can lower it.
  • Negative Body Image - Negative body image can lead to low self-esteem while positive body image can boost it.
  • Supportive Environment - Supportive environment can boost our self-esteem while a negative environment can lower it.

Ways of building a healthy self-esteem for the promotion of social cohesion:
  • Practice Empathy - Empathy is the ability to understand and share the feelings of others. When you develop empathy, it helps you connect with different people and promotes understanding and kindness. By putting yourself in someone else's shoes, you can build stronger relationships and contribute to social cohesion.
  • Embrace Diversity - Recognize and appreciate the diversity around you. Each person is unique and brings different experiences, perspectives, and cultures. By embracing diversity, you can foster an inclusive environment where everyone feels valued and respected.
  • Show Respect - Treat others with respect, regardless of their background, beliefs, or abilities. Respect involves listening to others, valuing their opinions, and treating them with kindness. When you show respect, it encourages mutual respect and cooperation, leading to social cohesion.
  • Engage in Positive Communication - Communicate with others in a positive and constructive manner. Be mindful of your words and actions, and strive for open and honest communication. Listen attentively, express yourself clearly, and seek to understand different viewpoints. Positive communication promotes understanding and fosters positive relationships.
  • Collaborate and Cooperate - Work together with others towards common goals. Collaboration and cooperation involve sharing ideas, supporting each other, and working as a team. By actively participating in group activities and projects, you contribute to social cohesion and create a sense of belonging.
  • Stand Up Against Bullying - Bullying and exclusion can harm individuals and weaken social cohesion. Take a stand against bullying by speaking up for those who are being mistreated and by promoting kindness and acceptance. Be a supportive friend and ally to those who need it.
  • Believing in Yourself - Believing in yourself and embracing your strengths and weaknesses.
  • Celebrating Successes - Celebrating successes and achievements.
  • Maintaining a Positive Attitude - Maintaining a positive attitude.
  • Being Kind to Yourself - Being kind to yourself by doing things you enjoy, practicing positive self-talk among others.
  • Promoting Body Positivity - Promoting body positivity and healthy body image.
  • Being Assertive - Being assertive.

Healthy esteem in social interactions:
  • Be Yourself - Embrace who you are and be authentic in your interactions. Don't try to be someone you're not just to fit in or please others. Accept yourself for who you are and let your true personality shine through.
  • Practice Active Listening - Show genuine interest in what others have to say. Give them your full attention, maintain eye contact, and listen without interrupting. This shows that you value their thoughts and opinions, and it helps build stronger connections with others.
  • Express Your Opinions - Don't be afraid to share your thoughts and ideas. Speak up respectfully and assertively when you have something to contribute to the conversation. Your opinions matter and expressing them shows that you have confidence in your own voice.
  • Set Boundaries - It's important to establish boundaries in your relationships. Know your limits and communicate them clearly and respectfully. By setting boundaries, you demonstrate that you value your own well-being and expect to be treated with respect.
  • Practice Positive Body Language - Your body language plays a significant role in how others perceive you. Stand tall, make eye contact, and maintain an open and relaxed posture. Smile genuinely and use friendly gestures. Positive body language can make you appear confident and approachable.
  • Be Kind and Respectful - Treat others with kindness, empathy, and respect. Show appreciation for their strengths and unique qualities. Being kind and respectful fosters positive relationships and encourages others to treat you the same way.
  • Embrace Mistakes and Learn from Them - Nobody is perfect, and it's okay to make mistakes. Instead of beating yourself up over them, view mistakes as opportunities for growth and learning. Accept constructive feedback gracefully and use it to improve yourself.
  • Celebrate Others' Success - Be genuinely happy for the accomplishments of others. Instead of feeling jealous or competitive, celebrate their achievements. Supporting others shows that you have a secure sense of self and that you value the success of those around you.
  • Being Confident in Ourselves - Being confident in ourselves.
  • Setting Goals - Setting goals and working towards achieving them.
  • Seeking Support - Seeking support or guidance when faced with challenges.
  • Handling Criticism Positively - Handling criticism positively.
  • Practicing Self-Care - Practicing self-care such as maintaining personal hygiene and doing physical exercise.

Values that enhance healthy self-esteem in day-to-day life:
  • Self-Respect - Value yourself and treat yourself with respect. Recognize your worth and make choices that align with your values and aspirations. When you respect yourself, others are more likely to respect you too.
  • Kindness - Practice kindness towards yourself and others. Be compassionate, understanding, and supportive. Small acts of kindness can boost your self-esteem and create positive connections with those around you.
  • Integrity - Integrity is the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles. It involves consistency in actions, values, methods, measures, and principles, ensuring that one's behaviors are aligned with ethical and moral standards. Act with integrity by being honest, trustworthy, and true to your values. When you live with integrity, you feel good about yourself and earn the respect of others.
  • Gratitude - Cultivate an attitude of gratitude by focusing on the positive aspects of your life. Regularly express appreciation for the things you have and the people who support you. Gratitude helps you maintain a positive outlook and boosts your self-esteem.
  • Authenticity - Be true to yourself and embrace your unique qualities. Emphasize your strengths and embrace your individuality rather than trying to fit in or be someone you're not. Embracing authenticity allows you to build genuine connections with others and boosts your self-confidence.
  • Resilience - Value resilience, which is the ability to bounce back from challenges and setbacks. View failures as learning opportunities and believe in your ability to overcome difficulties. Resilience strengthens your self-esteem and helps you face future challenges with confidence.
  • Collaboration - Value collaboration and teamwork. Recognize the strengths and contributions of others and work together towards common goals. Collaboration fosters a sense of belonging and strengthens your self-esteem through positive interactions.
  • Self-Care - Prioritize self-care by taking care of your physical, emotional, and mental well-being. Engage in activities that bring you joy, practice healthy habits, and prioritize rest and relaxation. When you take care of yourself, you show yourself that you value your own well-being.
  • Being Responsible - Responsibility is the state or quality of being accountable for something within one's power, control, or management. It involves the obligation to perform tasks, make decisions, and take ownership of the outcomes.
  • Being Patient - Patience is the capacity to accept or tolerate delay, trouble, or suffering without getting angry or upset. It involves maintaining a calm and composed demeanor in the face of challenges, setbacks, or prolonged periods of waiting.
  • Love - Love is a complex set of emotions, behaviors, and beliefs associated with strong feelings of affection, warmth, and respect towards another person or thing. It can manifest in various forms and contexts, such as romantic love, familial love, platonic love, and self-love.
  • Being Kind - Kindness is the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate. It involves acting with compassion and empathy towards others, often putting their needs and well-being before your own.
  • Being Respectful - Respect is the regard and consideration shown towards someone or something, recognizing their value, worth, and rights. It involves treating others with dignity and honor, and behaving in a way that acknowledges their inherent value.
