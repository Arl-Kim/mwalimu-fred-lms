Fractions
Introduction
  • A fraction is written in the form a/b where a and b are numbers and b is not equal to 0. The upper number is called the numerator and the lower number is the denominator.
  • a → numerator
  • b → denominator

Proper Fraction

In a proper fraction, the numerator is smaller than the denominator. E.g. ⅔, ¼


Improper Fraction
  • The numerator is bigger than or equal to the denominator. E.g. 7/3, 15/6, 9/2

Mixed Fraction
  • An improper fraction written as the sum of an integer and a proper fraction. For example: 7/3 = 2 + ⅓
    = 2⅓
