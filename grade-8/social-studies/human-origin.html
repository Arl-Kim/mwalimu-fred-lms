TOPIC 2: SCIENTIFIC THEORY ABOUT HUMAN ORIGIN

The theory of evolution explains how humans and other living organisms have evolved over time from simpler forms of life. It suggests that we share a common ancestry with other primates and have adapted to different environments through a gradual process. Scientific evidence from fossils, DNA analysis, and comparative anatomy supports this theory.

Understanding human evolution helps us appreciate our shared origins and the inter-connectedness of all living beings.

The Scientific Theory About Human Origin

The scientific theory about human origin is called the theory of evolution. It was proposed by a scientist named Charles Darwin in the 19th century. This theory explains how humans and other living organisms have developed and changed over a very long time.

According to the theory of evolution, all living things, including humans, share a common ancestor. This means that we have evolved from simpler forms of life over millions of years. It's like a big family tree, where all living organisms are connected in some way.

The theory of evolution suggests that living things change and adapt to their environment through a process called natural selection. This means that certain traits or characteristics that are helpful for survival and reproduction become more common in a population over time.

For example, if a certain trait helps an animal find food or avoid predators, individuals with that trait are more likely to survive and pass it on to their offspring.

Scientists have found a lot of evidence to support the theory of evolution. They study things like fossils (remains of ancient plants and animals), DNA (the genetic material that carries information about our traits), and similarities in the body structures of different organisms.

The Scientific Theory About Human Origin is Also Called the Evolution Theory

It was developed by Charles Darwin.

The theory argues that all living organisms underwent processes of change over millions of years by evolving from simple forms to complex forms.

This happened through the processes of:

  • Mutation - Human organisms went through changes in their genes and the behavior of their bodies.
  • Natural Selection - After mutation, only the fittest or strongest organisms survived.
  • Adaptation - After surviving mutation and natural selection, the organisms adapted to their new environment.

Stages of Human Evolution

The following are stages of human evolution:

  • Aegyptopithecus
    • Was the earliest ancestor of both human beings and apes.
    • Was discovered in Egypt.
    • Moved on four limbs.
    • Had a tail.

  • Dryopithecus
    • Had a flat skull and larger canines.
    • Was discovered in Rusinga Island.
    • Moved on four limbs.
    • Had a pointed face.

  • Kenyapithecus or Ramapithecus
    • Had larger teeth and jawbone.
    • Was discovered in Fort Tenan.
    • Moved on two legs.
    • Had a larger brain capacity.

  • Australopithecus
    • Had a loping forehead, projecting face, and big eyebrows.
    • Fossils were discovered in Ethiopia and Kenya.
    • Walked upright and had a larger brain capacity than Kenyapithecus.

  • Homo habilis (Handy Man)
    • Was human-like but with longer arms.
    • They lived in families.
    • Had a brain capacity larger than that of Australopithecus.
    • Was discovered in Olduvai Gorge in Tanzania.

  • Homo erectus
    • Was upright man.
    • Was clever with a brain capacity of 850cm3 which was larger than that of Homo habilis.
    • Discovered fire and made advanced tools.

  • Homo sapiens
    • They were more intelligent than the Homo erectus.
    • Had a brain capacity of 1000-1800cm3.
    • Had straight legs and walked with long strides.
    • Back of the skull was rounded with square eye sockets.

  • Homo sapiens sapiens
    • Was the last stage of human evolution.
    • Is the most advanced creature to appear on earth.
