Temporary and Permanent Changes in Substances
Temporary Physical Changes
Experiment to Demonstrate Physical Change
Requirements:
  • A pair of tongs
  • An iron pin
  • A source of heat such as a burning candle or Bunsen burner
  • Writing materials

Procedure:
  • Hold the iron pin using a pair of tongs over a burning flame from either a candle or Bunsen burner for some time.
  • Remove the pin from the flame after noticing any changes you can observe.



Observations, Explanation & Conclusion:
  • The iron pin becomes red-hot when heated.
  • After some time, the iron pin regains its original color on cooling.
  • This indicates that the iron pin went through a temporary change on heating.
  • Any change in properties such as shape, size, color, and state of a substance is called a physical change.
  • Physical changes are reversible.
  • When a change is reversible, it is said to be temporary. This is demonstrated when the heated red-hot iron pin regains its original color after cooling.
