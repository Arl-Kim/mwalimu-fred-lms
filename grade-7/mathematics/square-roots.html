Square Roots

Square roots are the opposite of squares. For example, 5 × 5 = 25, we say that 5 is a square root of 25.

Any positive number has two square roots, one positive and the other negative. The symbol for the square root of a number is √.

A number whose square root is an integer is called a perfect square. For example, 1, 4, 9, 25, and 36 are perfect squares.

Square Roots by Factorization

The square root of a number can also be obtained using the factorization method.

Example

Find the square root of 81 by factorization method.

√81 = 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 (Find the prime factors of 81)

(3 × 3) (3 × 3) (Group the prime factors into two identical numbers)

3 × 3 (Out of the two identical prime factors, choose one and find their product)

= 9

Note

Pair the prime factors into two identical numbers. For every pair, pick only one number then obtain the product.

Example

Find √1764 by factorization.

1764 = 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 × 7 × 7

= 2 × 3 × 7

= 42

Example

Find √441 by factorization.

√441 = 3 × 3 × 7 × 7

= 3 × 7

= 21

Square Root from Tables

Square roots of numbers from 1.0 to 99.99 are given in the tables and can be read directly.

Examples

Use tables to find the square root of:

  1. 1.86
  2. 42.57
  3. 359
  4. 0.8236

Solution
  1. To read the square root of 1.86, look for 1.8 in the column headed x, move to the right along this row to where it intersects with the column headed 6. The number in this position is the square root of 1.86. Thus 1.86 = 1.364 to 4 figures.

  2. For 42.57, look for 42 in the column headed x and move along the row containing 42 to where it intersects with the column headed 5. Read the number in this position, which is 6.519. The difference for 7 from the difference column along this row is 6. The difference is added to 6.519 as shown below:
    6.519 + 0.006 = 6.525
    Thus, √42.57 = 6.525 to 4 figures.

  3. For 359, express it as the product of a number in this range and an even power of 10: 359 = 3.59 × 10².
    Thus, √359 = √(3.59 × 10²) = 1.895 × 10 = 18.95 (four figures).

  4. For 0.8236, express it as 82.36 × (1/10)².
    Thus, √0.8236 = √(82.36 × 1/100) = (9.072 + 0.004) × 1/10 = 0.9076 (4 figures).
