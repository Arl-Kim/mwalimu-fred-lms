Location of Fixed Storage Devices in a Computer

Some fixed storage devices in a computer, such as the hard disk and solid-state drive, are usually found in the drive bay of the computer case or on the underside of a laptop. Some solid-state drives are embedded on the motherboard. Internal flash drives are mostly located in small portable devices such as mobile phones and cameras.

How Data is Stored (Written) and Retrieved (Read) from Fixed Storage Devices

Writing data or saving data means storing data. Data is written on a fixed storage device by:

  • Creating new files.
  • Downloading.
  • Copying or moving files and saving them in a computer storage device.

Reading data, retrieving data, or opening data is the same as getting data back from its location. When data is opened or a file is accessed in a fixed storage device, then the data is read.
