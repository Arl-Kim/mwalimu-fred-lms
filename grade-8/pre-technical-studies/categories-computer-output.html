Categories of Computer Output Devices
Soft-copy Output Devices

They give intangible output in the form of text, audio, and videos. These devices include:

  • Monitors
  • Speakers
  • Projectors

Hard-copy Output Devices

They give tangible output that is in the form of printed text or graphics. Examples of hardcopy output devices are:

  • Printers
  • Plotters

Advantages and Disadvantages of Soft-copy and Hard-copy Output
Type Advantages Disadvantages
Hard-copy
  • It is considered permanent data as editing and changing is not easy.
  • It does not need electricity, special devices, or software to display/show.
  • When properly stored, it is not easily lost.
  • It is not subjected to data stealing and cyber attacks.
  • It is expensive to produce as it requires paper, ink, and a printer.
  • It is not easy to move from one place to another.
  • It requires a lot of physical storage space.
  • It can be stolen or destroyed through wear and tear or be subjected to factors such as fire and water.
Soft-copy
  • It is easy to move from one place to another.
  • It is cheap to produce as it does not require paper or ink.
  • Large amounts of data and information can be stored without the need for a lot of physical space.
  • It is beneficial to the environment as it reduces the number of trees cut to make paper.
  • It is vulnerable to cyber and data stealing.
  • It requires electricity, a device, and software to be displayed or shown.
  • It is considered temporary data which can be altered or manipulated.
